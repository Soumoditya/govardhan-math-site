The Founder

Bhagavatpada Adi Shankaracharya

Around 507 B.C. there descended on the sacred, deeply adorable soil of this holy land a personality extraordinary who by the sheer force of his matchless erudition, enlightened the whole world. He was none other than Acharya Adi Shankara, a very embodiment of Lord Shiva, tenth in the line of Guru traceable back to Sriman Narayana himself.

His Holiness was born in Kaladi village of Kerala. His father was Shivaguru and mother was Sati Aryamba. He was a Nambudaripad Brahmin. Born on Yudhisthir Sambat 2631 (507 BC) on Vaishakha Sukla Panchami, Sunday.

Life & Accomplishments

His thread ceremony took place at the age of 5 years. Taking his mother's blessings, he left home to take a life of Sanyasa, initiated by Shree Govindapadacharya on the auspicious day in the Kartik month on Devothana Ekadashi.

From the age of 5 to 8 years, Acharya Shankara learnt the Vedas, Vedanga, Dharma Shastra, Purana, Itihasa and Budhagama. According to Sanatana Dharma and Shiva Purana, Hindus call Adi Shankaracharya as the reincarnation of Lord Shiva.

Within a short span of sixteen years, he re-established Sanatana Dharma after overcoming Buddhism and other directionless philosophies. He travelled far and wide, preaching the Vedic Sanatana Dharma across the entire subcontinent.

He wrote commentaries on the Prasthanatrayishastras. He defeated Buddhist scholars in philosophical debates. He also defeated scholars like Sri Mandana Mishra by his command of shastras and made him his disciple.

He grouped his disciples into ten categories ŌĆö Vana, Parvata, Aranya, Tirtha, Ashrama, Giri, Puri, Bharati, Sagar and Saraswati (the Dasanami Sampradaya). These sanyasis were tasked to propagate dharmic values in their assigned regions.

His holy body departed at the age of 32 years in 475 BC on the holy day of Kartika Poornima.

145th Shankaracharya

Swami Nischalananda Saraswati

The current Shankaracharya was born on 30th June 1943 in a village called Haripur Bakshi Tol in the Madhubani district of Bihar. His father was Pandit Sri Lalvamshi Jha ŌĆö a high-ranking scholar of Sanskrit in the Mithila tradition and a court vidwan of the then king of Mithila (Darbhanga Kingdom). His mother was Smt Gita Devi.

His previous name was Nilambar, given by his elder brother Pandit Sridev Jha. In childhood, Nilambar was bestowed with amazing character and was very studious and intelligent. His early education took place in Kaluahi, Loha, and Delhi.

Jagadguru Shankaracharya Swami Nischalananda Saraswati Ji Maharaj

Spiritual Journey

Age 17

Left home and started his spiritual journey. In a seminar involving all branches of Vedas, Dharma Samrat Karpatri-ji Maharaj was present. He accepted Karpatri-ji Maharaj as his spiritual master.

Given Name

Named Dhruvachaitanya by Swami Sri Naradanand Saraswati of Naimisharanya. Studied Vedas at Kashi, Vrindavan, Naimisharanya, Badrikashram, Rishikesh, Haridwar, Puri, Sringeri.

9 Nov 1966

Fought against atrocities on cows, participating in Dharma Samrat Karpatriji Maharaj's agitation for cow protection. Kept in Tihar jail for 52 days for advocating cow protection.

18 April 1974

Accepted the sanyasa order from Swami Sri Karpatriji Maharaj on Vaisakha Krishna Ekadashi of Samvat 2031 in Haridwar. Initiated under the new name Nischalananda Saraswati.

1976ŌĆō1981

Studied Prasthanatrayi, Panchadasi, Vedanta Paribhasha, Nyaya Mimamsa, Tantra and Advaita Siddhi from his Gurudev.

1982ŌĆō1987

Studied Khandanakhanda Khadya and Yajur Veda from the then Shankaracharya of Puri, Swami Sri Niranjanadev Tirtha ji Maharaj. Spent five Chaturmasas with him.

9 Feb 1992

Impressed by his talents, brilliance, and dedication, Shankaracharya Swami Sri Niranjanadev Tirtha ji Maharaj anointed him the 145th Shankaracharya of Govardhan Peetha at Puri.

Contributions & Outreach

The Shankaracharya of Puri has been travelling far and wide across India ŌĆö like the first guru Adi Shankaracharya ŌĆö to protect Sanatana Dharma, its values and glories. He addresses concerns on:

National Integration

He has set up Aditya Vahini and Anand Vahini under the aegis of 'Peeth Parishad' ŌĆö creating an intelligent, self-reliant society, protecting values, securing the nation. Through these organizations, he spearheads a nationwide campaign to protect India's ancient culture.
