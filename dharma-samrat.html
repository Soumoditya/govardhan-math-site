धर्म सम्राट

Dharma Samrat Swami Karpatri Ji Maharaj

स्वामी करपात्री जी महाराज — धर्म सम्राट

A luminary of Sanatana Dharma, towering Vedic scholar, fearless social reformer, and a beloved figure revered by the Shankaracharya tradition — Swami Karpatri Ji Maharaj embodied the living spirit of Vaishnava-Shaiva unity in India.

Dharma Samrat Swami Karpatri Ji Maharaj
Background

The Saint Who Lived with Nothing

Born as Harihar Narayan Ojha in 1907, in a Brahmin family in Pratapgarh, Uttar Pradesh, his extraordinary intellect and spiritual inclination were apparent from childhood. He mastered Sanskrit, the Vedas, Upanishads, and Shastras at a remarkably young age.

Renouncing all worldly possessions including cooking utensils (the word "Karpatri" comes from kara = hand and patra = vessel — he used only his hands as his bowl), he became one of the most celebrated parivrajaka (wandering) monks of India.

"Dharma is not merely a religion — it is the eternal cosmic law that sustains existence. To abandon it is to invite the collapse of all civilization."
— Swami Karpatri Ji Maharaj

His title "Dharma Samrat" (Emperor of Dharma) was bestowed upon him by the Shankaracharyas of all four Amnaya Peethams and hundreds of sages in recognition of his unparalleled service to Sanatana Dharma.

Timeline

Life & Mission

1907

Birth in Pratapgarh

Born as Harihar Narayan Ojha to a learned Brahmin family in Uttar Pradesh.

~1920s

Sannyas Diksha

Received Sannyas from Swami Vishuddhananda Saraswati of Kashi. Took the vow of complete renunciation.

1940

Founded Dharma Sangha

Established the Dharma Sangha, a monastic-scholarly organization dedicated to the protection and propagation of Vedic Dharma.

1948

Ram Rajya Parishad

Founded the Ram Rajya Parishad political party to contest elections and uphold Dharmic governance.

1966

Cow Protection Movement

Led the massive Gohatya Bandh Andolan — the historic cow protection movement in Delhi involving hundreds of saints and millions of followers.

1982

Mahasamadhi

Attained Mahasamadhi on February 7, 1982, in Kashi (Varanasi).

Contributions

Defender of Sanatana Dharma

Decades of fearless service to the ancient heritage of Bharat

Vedic Scholarship

Mastered all six Vedangas, Nyaya, Mimamsa, Vedanta, Vyakarana, and the principal Upanishads. Authored numerous Shastra commentaries.

Cow Protection

A lifelong crusader for Gau Raksha. Led the historic 1966 Delhi march — the largest-ever assembly of saints — demanding a constitutional ban on cow slaughter.

Ram Mandir Advocacy

Among the earliest and most persistent voices demanding the restoration of the Ram Janmabhoomi temple at Ayodhya — decades before the Supreme Court verdict.

Unity of Saints

Brought together saints from all sampradayas — Vaishnava, Shaiva, Shakta — under one platform. His Dharma Sangha continues to hold annual sants-sammelans.

Literary Legacy

Authored seminal works including Marxwad aur Ramrajya, Vedo ka Rahasya, and extensive commentaries on the Bhagavad Gita and Brahma Sutras.

Political Dharma

Founded the Ram Rajya Parishad political party and strongly opposed policies that would undermine Hindu religious law. Organized massive satyagrahas.

Sacred Connection

Association with Govardhan Math

Deep Fraternal Respect

Swami Karpatri Ji maintained deep respect and association with the Shankaracharyas of Govardhan Math, Puri. He considered the Govardhan Peetham a sacred seat of the Advaita Vedanta tradition and cooperated closely with the then Shankaracharyas on matters of Dharma, Vedic tradition, and national concerns.

On many occasions, he participated in the joint sants-sammelans convened by Govardhan Math and Kashi — particularly on issues of Gau Raksha and Ram Janmabhoomi.

Title "Dharma Samrat"

The title Dharma Samrat was formally conferred upon Swami Karpatri Ji by all four Shankaracharyas of the Amnaya Peethams — Sringeri, Dwaraka, Badrinath (Jyotirmath), and Govardhan Math, Puri — at a grand assembly of thousands of saints and scholars.

The conferral by the Govardhan Math Peethadhipati acknowledged Swami Karpatri Ji as the supreme defender of dharma in the modern era.
