The Sacred Timeline

Tracing the roots of the Eastern Pitham back to the 8th Century.

8th Century AD

Foundation by Adi Shankaracharya

Govardhan Math was established by the great philosopher-saint Adi Shankaracharya. It is one of the four cardinal institutions (Amnaya Pithams) created to revive and protect Sanatana Dharma across the Indian subcontinent.

The Eastern Region

Spiritual Jurisdiction

The Math provides spiritual guidance to the eastern region, historically encompassing Odisha, West Bengal, Bihar, parts of the Northeast, Nepal, and Bangladesh. Its tradition is deeply rooted in the Rigveda.

Deities Installed

Govardhanath Krishna & Ardhanareshvara Shiva

Adi Shankaracharya installed the deities of Govardhanath Krishna and Ardhanareshvara Shiva. The Math also has a profound historical connection with the Jagannath Temple, with Lord Jagannath and Goddess Vimala as its presiding deities.
