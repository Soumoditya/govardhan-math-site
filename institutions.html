Our Institutions

Preserving Dharma through education, conservation, and community service

Ved Gurukulam

A traditional Vedic school dedicated to the meticulous preservation and oral transmission of the Rigveda. Young students (Brahmacharis) live in an authentic ashram environment, learning Sanskrit, scriptures, Vedic recitation, and traditional rituals to become the future torchbearers of Sanatana Dharma.

The Gurukulam provides education from elementary Vedic chanting to advanced Shastric studies, maintaining the ancient Guru-Shishya parampara in its purest form.

Shree Govardhan Gaushala

Dedicated to the protection, welfare, and sustenance of indigenous cows (Gomata). The Gaushala serves as a model for sustainable agriculture, utilizing Panchagavya (five products of the cow) for organic farming and producing medicines based on ancient Ayurvedic principles.

It aims to revive awareness of the central role of Gomata in Indian civilization and demonstrate the viability of cow-based sustainable living.

Aditya Vahini

A social organization working under the direct guidance of the Shankaracharya, focused on spiritual awakening, cultural preservation, and national service. It mobilizes youth across the country for constructive social engagement rooted in Dharmic values.

Anand Vahini

The women's wing of the Math's social initiatives. Anand Vahini empowers women through spiritual education, community service, and the preservation of Vedic cultural practices at the grassroots level across eastern India.

Swasti Prakashan Sansthanam

The official publication house of Govardhan Math, responsible for printing and distributing books, discourses, and treatises on Vedanta, Vedic Mathematics, and Indian philosophy authored by the Shankaracharyas.

Shankaracharya Library

A repository of rare manuscripts, ancient texts, and philosophical treatises. The library supports scholars, researchers, and seekers in their pursuit of Vedic knowledge, philosophy, Indology, and the history of Indian mathematics.
