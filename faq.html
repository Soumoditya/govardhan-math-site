FAQ & Visitor Guide

Everything you need to know before visiting Govardhan Math, Puri

Information

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Govardhan Math?

+
Govardhan Math (also spelled Govardhana Matha) is one of the four cardinal Amnaya Peethams established by Adi Shankaracharya. It is the Poorvamnaya (Eastern) seat, located in Puri, Odisha. It is associated with the Rigveda, the Mahavakya "Prajñānam Brahma", and has Lord Jagannath as the presiding deity.

Who is the current Shankaracharya?

+
The current and 145th Shankaracharya of Govardhan Math is Jagadguru Shankaracharya Swami Nischalananda Saraswati Ji Maharaj. He was born on 30th June 1943 in Darbhanga, Bihar, and was coronated on 9th February 1992.

Can visitors visit Govardhan Math?

+
Yes, Govardhan Math is open to visitors. Devotees can visit during regular darshan hours. The Math is located on Swargadwara Road, Puri, near the Sri Jagannath Temple. Visitors should dress modestly and follow the decorum of a sacred monastery. Photography restrictions may apply in certain areas.

How to reach Govardhan Math, Puri?

+
By Air: Nearest airport is Biju Patnaik International Airport, Bhubaneswar (~60 km).
By Rail: Puri Railway Station is well-connected to major cities. The Math is ~2 km from the station.
By Road: Puri is connected via NH-16 and NH-316. The Math is on Swargadwara Road, Shankaracharya Marg.

What are the four Amnaya Peethams?

+
Poorvamnaya (East): Govardhan Math, Puri — Rigveda
Dakshinamnaya (South): Sringeri Sharada Peetham — Yajurveda
Pashchimamnaya (West): Dwaraka Sharada Peetham — Samaveda
Uttaramnaya (North): Jyotirmath, Badrinath — Atharvaveda

What is the Mahavakya of Govardhan Math?

+
"प्रज्ञानं ब्रह्म" (Prajñānam Brahma) — "Consciousness is Brahman." This is from the Aitareya Upanishad of the Rigveda.

What is the connection between Govardhan Math and Vedic Mathematics?

+
The 143rd Shankaracharya, Bharati Krishna Teerthaji (1884–1960), is world-famous for rediscovering 16 Vedic Sutras for rapid mathematical calculations. His book "Vedic Mathematics" (1965) is a global bestseller. The current Shankaracharya has written 11 books on Vedic Mathematics and was invited by ISRO to share Vedic formulas for Chandrayaan-2.

What are the Dasanami titles of Govardhan Math?

+
Sannyasis of Govardhan Math bear the Dasanami titles 'Vana' and 'Aranya'. The Brahmachari (pre-initiation) title is 'Prakash'.

How many Shankaracharyas has Govardhan Math had?

+
Govardhan Math has had 145 Shankaracharyas in an unbroken lineage spanning over 2,500 years, from Padmapadacharya (484 BCE) to the current Shankaracharya Swami Nischalananda Saraswati (1992–present). View the complete Guruparampara.
